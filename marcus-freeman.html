Marcus Freeman is a famous American football coach and a former American footballer. Marcus Freeman is now the head coach of the University of Notre Dame since January 2021.

Early Life

Marcus Freeman was born on January 10, 1986, and he is currently 36 years old. Freeman hails from Dayton, Ohio, and his zodiac sign is Capricorn. This personality was born at the Wright-Patterson Air Force Base Medical Center in Fairborn, Ohio.

He talks about his family, his parents Michael Freeman and Chong. His parents met each other when his father was serving in the Air Force in South Korea. Similarly, he has an elder brother named Michael Jr.

Marcus Freeman – Educational Background

When his family lived in Huber Heights, Freeman attended Wayne High School. Similarly, Freeman was named to the Parade All-America team coming out of Wayne High School.

Additionally, this personality also ran track, competed in the 4×100-meter relay, as well as threw the shot and discus. Later on, he went on to attend Ohio State University from 2004 to 2008.

Career and Professional Life

Professionally, Marcus Freeman is a popular American football coach. Previously, he was also a linebacker. At present, this personality is the head coach at the University of Notre Dame. Previously, he also served as the defensive coordinator and linebackers coach at the same institution in the year 2021.

Likewise, he was previously an assistant coach at various institutions including the University of Cincinnati, Kent State University, Purdue University, and Ohio State University.

More Details

As a former player, this personality played college football at Ohio State. Later, the Chicago Bears drafted this personality in the 5th round of the 2009 NFL Draft. Similarly, Freeman has also been a member of the Buffalo Bills and Houston Texans.

While attending Ohio State University, this personality appeared in 51 games throughout his career. He then signed with the Buffalo Bills practice squad on September 22, 2009, and was later released in early October of the same year.

Then on 4 November 2009, this personality signed with the Houston Texan. Additionally, Freeman retired as a player on 1 May 2010 due to an enlarged heart condition.
