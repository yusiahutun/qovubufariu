The CMA Announces Additional Acts For Fan Fest, Including Old Dominion, Gretchen Wilson &amp; Terri Clar

NASHVILLE, TN (CelebrityAccess) — The Country Music Association announced an additional round of performers ahead of the 2024 CMA Fest, which lands at multiple stages in and around downtown Nashville from June 6-9.

The list of newly announced artists includes Terri Clark, Craig Morgan, Josh Turner and Gretchen Wilson, who are slated to open the night concerts at Nissan Stadium during the festival.

Additionally, Old Dominion has joined the lineup for the event’s main stage and fans can enjoy performances at the stadium from Jackson Dean, Shaboozey and Brittney Spencer, while the venue’s Platform Stage will feature Tanner Adell, Chapel Hart, Dasha, Wyatt Flores, Dylan Gossett, Dylan Marlowe, Puddin (K. Michelle) and Zach Top.

The fest will also see the return of the Tennessee State University marching band, The Aristocrat of Bands, who, for the second year in a row will welcome CMA Fest fans to Nashville Thursday morning as they march across the John Seigenthaler Pedestrian Bridge.

Other acts announced for the festival’s various stages for 2024 include Julia Cole, Allie Colleen, Shelby Darrall, Exile, Chris Housman, Willie Jones, Tiera Kennedy, Love and Theft, Dylan Marlowe, Matt Schuster, Sister Hazel, The Chattahoochies and Louie TheSinger.

The MA Close Up Stage inside Fan Fair X will feature a special Q&A session with country legend Gretchen Wilson as she 20 years of her hit single “Redneck Woman.”

On festival’s Spotlight Stage inside Fan Fair X, Color Me Country will host a writer’s round featuring Alyssia Dominguez, O.N.E The Duo, David Unlayao and Michael B. Whit, while the Canadian Country Music Association will present a writer’s round featuring Robert Adam, Zach McPhee and Trudy.

All artist lineups are subject to change. Artists performing at CMA Fest donate their time so a portion of proceeds can directly benefit music education initiatives across the United States through the CMA Foundation.
