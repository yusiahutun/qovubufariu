Sky Sports F1 confirm major punditry shake-up ahead of Australian Grand Prix

Sky Sports F1 have made a big change to its Australian Grand Prix TV broadcast, halving the number of paddock pundits.

For the next three races, starting this weekend Down Under, only four of the regular team will be in the paddock. The others will present from the broadcaster's main studio in Osterley, west London.

The studio is described as a "state-of-the-art" facility which is "surrounded by high resolution screens and immersive AR graphic - seamlessly blending cutting edge set design and best-in-class innovative technologies".

The studio will be familiar to viewers of the broadcaster's popular Monday Night Football programme, during which its hi-tech features are used to help with the in-depth analysis associated with the show.

The SkyPad system, which is usually set up in the paddock to analyse on-board footage from cars, will feature as start of the new studio set-up. It also boasts a large LED floor which the broadcaster says "will allow the presentation team to explain track maps in an innovative and entertaining new way", reports the Mirror.

The change has been made in part because of the geographical reality of the next few races on the F1 calendar. The sport has set up shop in Melbourne this week, on the other side of the world, while the next two rounds in Japan and China are also long-haul races.

In the past, Sky has usually taken a team of eight presenters and pundits to most races. But for the next three events, only four will be in the paddock with the main coverage being presented from the Osterley studio.

For the Australian Grand Prix, commentator David Croft will be in Melbourne with pit lane reporter Ted Kravitz, presenter Rachel Brookes and pundit Martin Brundle. Natalie Pinkham, Naomi Schiff and Karun Chandhok will complete the broadcasting team for the weekend.

Croft and Kravitz will also be at the races in Japan and China. At Suzuka, they will be joined by Brookes and former F1 driver Anthony Davidson. Pinkham will again present from the studio, this time with 1996 world champion Damon Hill and former McLaren and Aston Martin strategist Bernie Collins.

For the Shanghai race, Croft and Kravitz will be joined in China by ex-Mercedes racer Nico Rosberg and Sky Sports News F1 correspondent Craig Slater. Main presenter Simon Lazenby will lead the studio team, joined by Hill and Chandhok.
