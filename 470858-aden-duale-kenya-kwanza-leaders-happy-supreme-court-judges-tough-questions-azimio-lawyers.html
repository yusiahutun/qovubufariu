Aden Duale, Kenya Kwanza Leaders Happy with Supreme Court Judges Tough Questions to Azimio Lawyers

On Wednesday, August 31, the petitioners in the 2022 presidential election petition argued their case before the Supreme Court judges.

In their submission, they explained to the seven-judge bench how the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission bungled the presidential election.

However, the judges took the legal teams representing the petitioners to task regarding the evidence to support their submissions.

Questions on 4 IEBC commissioners

To begin with, Justice Isaac Lenaola questioned the last-minute rebellion by the four IEBC commissioners led by vice chair Juliana Cherera.

"We saw your clients in public reading out results until the eleventh hour, what evidence do we have that this was an afterthought?" Lenaola posed a question to lawyer Jothan Arwa.

How hackers could edit form 34As

The Supreme Court judges questioned a forms 34As could be edited and still maintains their handwriting.

Justice Smokin Wanjala wondered how hackers could change the physical forms 34As being delivered by road to the Bomas of Kenya.

Raila Odinga’s legal team had alleged that forms 34A from polling stations were being intercepted and edited before being uploaded to the IEBC website.

The tough questions by the apex court judges to Azimio lawyers attracted praise from Kenya Kwanza Alliance leaders.

Some took to social media to say the judges' questions were a clear indicator that Azimio had no strong case that would warrant nullification of William Ruto's win.
