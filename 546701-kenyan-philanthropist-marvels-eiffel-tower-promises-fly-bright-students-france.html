Kenyan Philanthropist Marvels at Eiffel Tower, Promises to Fly Bright Students to France

  • The Eiffel Tower was constructed between 1887 and 1889 and is the world's most visited paid-for monument
  • The iconic tower is made from wrought iron, is 312 metres tall, and has a base of at least 125 metres
  • Agnes Kagure, who the Eiffel Tower experience awed, promised to fly Kenyan students to the historic monument to inspire them to dream big

Michael Ollinga is a journalist at TUKO.co.ke with over 10 years of experience covering courts and crimes, special reports, and current affairs in Kenya.

A Kenyan philanthropist could not hide her astonishment after visiting one of the world's historic establishments in France.

What is the Eiffel Tower?

On her social media accounts, Agnes Kagure, who is also the patron of the Kenya University Students' Organisation (KUSO), shared her amazement at visiting France's global cultural icon, the Eiffel Tower.

The Eiffel Tower, named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed it in 1887, is made of wrought iron and is 312 metres tall and 125 metres wide.

TUKO.co.ke established that construction work on the tower that served as the entrance to the World's Fair began on January 26, 1887, and was completed on March 31, 1889.

According to toureiffel.paris, the tower's unique design, which was meticulously executed, makes it one of the most recognisable structures worldwide.

World's most visited paid-for monument

According to cometoparis.com, the Eiffel Tower is the world's most visited paid-for monument, registering an average of seven million visitors annually. It was listed as a historical monument in 1964.

These features justify Kagure's amazement at the Eiffel Tower after she visited the Champ-de-Mars.

She said the historical marvel was an inspiration to dream big for her and her country and vowed to help many other Kenyans experience the monument, assured that it would inspire them to chart innovation paths they were afraid of taking.

"From its breathtaking view to its intricate ironwork, every aspect of this architectural marvel tells a story of innovation and ingenuity. I can't help but appreciate the dedication and craftsmanship that went into its creation and wonder what we can achieve when we dare to dream big and push the boundaries of possibility," Kagure said.

How much does it cost to visit the Eiffel Tower?

The philanthropist, who sponsors the education of at least 38 students in primary, high school, and universities, vowed to sponsor brilliant Kenyan students to visit Paris, France, and experience the Eiffel Tower as part of the inspiration to dream big.

"In the coming days, my foundation will send bright school-going students under our sponsorship to such trips to give them a first-hand experience of what they can achieve when they pursue their goals," she said.

A ticket to access the Eiffel Tower ranges between KSh 2,711 and KSh 4241 for adults, depending on the height level
