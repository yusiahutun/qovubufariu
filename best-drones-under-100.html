The Holy Stone HS110D just beats the $100 threshold but is the best budget drone you can find in this price range. With its compact dimensions of 12.6 x 12.6 x 4.6 inches and a weight of 5.2 ounces, it’s perfect for travel and easily beats the 250-gram limit set by most countries for drone registration.

It’s easy to fly and is ideal for beginners, though small hands may struggle with the large controller. You can get the drone up to a reasonable speed, make quick turns, and do simple flips. You’ll get around eight minutes of flight time per battery, and the pack contains two.

There is also no tilt or pan, but beginners can use Altitude Hold Mode to get steady shots. Newbies can also fly in Headless Mode, and the drone even offers voice and gesture controls through the Deerc FPV app.

With its 1080p resolution, the camera on the SANROCK U52 is among the best for drones priced under $100. The Full HD camera also has a 90-degree adjustable angle giving you options of a bird’s-eye view or a higher landscape perspective. You also get FPV real-time transmissions, with footage automatically saved to your microSD card. From there, you can edit and upload to social media from your mobile device.

Flying the drone is also a lot of fun. Pilots can perform 360-degree flips in four directions, while you can control the drone by moving your smartphone thanks to the drone’s integrated G-Sensor. Furthermore, you can map out customized routes on the app, while it also allows for simple voice and gesture controls. These include verbal commands for launching and landing and hand gestures to activate photo and video functions.

Regarding safety features, the SANROCK U52 has an alarm system alerting you when the signal is weak or the battery is draining. The drone will return home with a simple button press or voice command. The maximum control range is up to 320 feet, which is reduced to 96 feet during live video transmissions. With the 1,500mAh battery providing up to 13 minutes of flight time, it’s important to note that charging takes up to three hours, and the pack doesn’t include a spare.

Key Factors to Consider When Purchasing a Drone Under $100

The primary consideration is that you will unlikely find a drone in this price range that offers incredible video quality. If you are willing to settle for a preowned drone, you might get lucky and find one with a 4K resolution at under $100. However, when buying new at this price point, the likelihood is that you will have to settle for 480 or 720p. Yet, there are some available that offer Full HD. One of the best is the SANROCK U52. On top of its 1080p video resolution, it is easy to use and has a decent flight time.

However, you might be looking for a drone under $100 suitable for children or beginners. Buying a low-cost drone with quality features can certainly alleviate fears of damaging expensive gear while you practice your piloting. Mini drones like the Force1 UFO 3000 or the Potensic A20 are tons of fun and pocket-friendly.

But, for an affordable all-rounder, you could try the Holy Stone HS110D. It has a camera with a 1080p resolution, a decent battery life, excellent features for novices, and is easy to fly.
