Career and Professional Life

Eric Weinstein is an American economist, podcast host, entrepreneur, and managing director of Thiel Capital. Later, he joined AdKap LLC as head of research in 2001 and joined Capital Market Risk Advisors as a consultant-at-large to study the transparency issues related to hedge funds with illiquid exposure. In addition, he served as an outside collaborator with the International Labor Office of the United Nations from 2000 to 2002.

In the Clarendon Laboratory at Oxford University, Weinstein delivered his two-hour lecture titled “Geometric Unity” on May 23, 2013.

His idea is based on mathematical equations and the cosmologically occurring geometric symmetry. According to Weinstein’s theory, our universe has 14 dimensions, including three for space and one for time.

He published a draft paper on geometric unity on The Joe Rogan Experience on April 1, 2021. He presented his concept for using gauge theory to compute economic inflation indices on November 10, 2021.

The scientific community harshly criticized it, claiming that it had “no discernible influence” and that there were “gaps both mathematical and physical in origin that endanger Geometric Unity as a well-defined theory.”
