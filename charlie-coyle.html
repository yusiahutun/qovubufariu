Early NHL Career- Amateur Years

As mentioned above, Coyle has been playing ice hockey since his childhood. Originally from Massachusetts, Charlie played for his high school team, the Wildcat varsity, during his freshman year.

During that time, through his help, the team made their first-ever MIAA appearance, played at the TD Garden against a private school, Boston College High School.

Likewise, the Wildcat Varsity came out as an underdog beating notable private schools like Maiden Catholic High School, Austin Preparatory School, and Central Catholic High School.

Before completing his senior year, Coyle also played for Thayer Academy in Braintree.

He then went on and played with the South Shore Kings against Tier III Junior A Ice hockey in the Eastern Junior Hockey League(EJHL).

During his first and only season, Charlie finished fifth overall, where he scored 63 points in 42 games.

After playing for various teams, young Coyle finally settled with NCAA Division I College ice hockey with the Boston University Terriers of the Hockey East for the 2010-11 season.

In their first game, Coyle scored two assists and finished the season with 26 points in a total of 37 regular games.

Keeping that in mind, Charlie finally decided to leave Boston University and joined Saint John Sea Dogs of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League(QMJHL) on December 16, 2011.

What position does Charlie Coyle play?- NHL

The young talent, Charlie Coyle, came into the NHL professional league after being drafted in the first round, 28th overall at the 2010 NHL Entry Draft.
