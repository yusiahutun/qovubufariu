Fan gets massive Nick Saban Alabama tattoo on his BACK

A man has become a viral sensation after a picture of his back tattoo of Alabama Crimson Tide's head coach Nick Saban was shared on social media.

Jason McMillan was enjoying his Saturday at the Geico 500 NASCAR race on the Talledega Superspeedway in Alabama when his back art was snapped by a racing fan and shared with FoxSports' Clay Travis.

'I came back from Talladega, and my Facebook was blowing up with notifications,' McMillan told AL.com on Wednesday night.

He had gotten the first part of the tattoo collage done by high school friend Byron Phillips in 2012 - it was just the logo and the title years. He would add the face - which took five hours to complete - four years later in 2016. 

Sports Analyst Clay Travis quickly shared the Muscle Shoals, Alabama native's tattoo in a Tweet with a caption that said: 'Bama fan at Talladega is rocking a Nick Saban back tat. Amazing.'

The Alabama Crimson Tide super fan doesn't plan to end his tribute to the football empire any time soon. 

He hopes to feature Paul 'Bear' Bryant on the left shoulder looking down on Bryant-Denny Stadium and wants to add the champion ship rings to the artwork. McMillan, who owns McMillions Body and Paint will also include Heisman trophy winners Mark Ingram and Derrick Henry. 

McMillan has always been a die hard Alabama fan all his life.

His mother, who died in 1999, 'never saw the tattoo' but played a huge influence as to why he came to love the team. 

'She used to wake me up with a Roll Tide Roll for those 11 o'clock games because they weren't real good back then,' said McMillan who has a 10-month-old pit bull named Bama. 

The fan tries to attend a game each season and hopes to eventually meet Nick Saban but it is clear what team he has no respect for. 

'We hate Tennessee,' he said. 'That damn Rocky Top. I can't stand that crap.
