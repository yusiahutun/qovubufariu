Tyson Fury reacts to brother Roman's fourth ever victory on Anthony Joshua undercard

Tyson Fury watched his brother Roman win the fourth fight of his career on the Anthony Joshua vs Francis Ngannou undercard.

Roman, 27, put on an assured display in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, on Friday night. He took on Martin Svarc, with the pair going all the way as the Gypsy King’s younger sibling was deemed the winner with the referees judging it 39-37 on the scorecard.

He moved to 4-0 with one previous knockout to his name, while brothers WBC world heavyweight boxing champion Tyson and Love Island winner Tommy cheered him on ringside. And Tyson was joined by his wife Paris alongside him as they clapped the victory.

The elder Fury could be seen grinning and beaming with pride after seeing Roman hand Svarc his first defeat (2-1). Roman had settled into the fight with jabs in the first round before taking control, his punches landing more cleanly than the Czech’s.

Svarc came back fighting with hard shots of his own but Fury withstood his power and hurt his opponent with a hard southpaw left in the third. And while Svarc refused to bow down, his nose was bloodied by the final round as it became clear Fury was the winner.

Roman said post-fight: "Every fight's a learning fight. It's a victory over an undefeated fighter, good learning fight. I'm enjoying it, I've been pro for two years now and I'm riding the wave."

"I feel the pressure but I am enjoying life. Where I've come from from two years ago to where I am now, nobody could have imagined it. I keep thinking I might wake up soon because all of this just feels like a dream.

"I am riding the wave, two years ago I was nowhere and I need to thank my father for training me. And thank you to Tyson for coming to watch me because it’s all happening thanks to him."

The Manchester-born cruiserweight is the son of John Fury and Chantal, while Tyson’s mum is Amber. That makes influencer and fellow boxer Tommy his full brother

Roman was striving to become a Rugby League player before taking boxing seriously. He revealed he switched careers after training with Tommy, who helped him out of a rut after he lost his job during the pandemic.
