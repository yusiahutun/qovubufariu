This is the moment that Jeremy Vine yelled at a bus driver and shouted 'what the f***' in an apparent near-miss in London.

The BBC star and cycling enthusiast complained he was nearly hit by the double decker turning into his lane at Holland Park roundabout - what has been called one of the capital's most dangerous junctions. 

He shared video captured by his 360-degree camera while cycling in west London, approaching the much-criticised roundabout.

The panicked presenter recorded his terror - yelling 'What the f***' - as a bus overtaking on his right began to edge into his lane - though they did not collide.

BBC Radio 2 presenter and Eggheads host Vine shared the clip with his 787,500 followers on X, Twitter - and now Transport for London has ordered an investigation.

Campaigners have long been calling for action to improve safety at Holland Park roundabout - but Transport for London's plans to add a cycle lane also have critics. TfL says it needs to act after 59 people - including 14 cyclists and pedestrians - were injured in collisions there in the three years to last May, including six seriously hurt.  

A proposed scheme would add a two-way cycle lane on the inner lane of the roundabout's southern edge between Holland Park Avenue and Shepherd's Bush Green, connecting with a cycle route to and from Wood Lane and TfL's Cycleway 34.

Cyclists would be given their own traffic lights to help cross the junction.

Vine told MailOnline this afternoon: 'Thank God they are finally doing something about this awful roundabout. At the moment it's only the bravest who cycle there, and it should be for everyone.'

This morning's incident is the latest captured on 360-degree camera by cycling champion Vine, who often uses his social media to 'shame' motorists. There was a mixed response to his latest post.

A commenter told the presenter: 'Luckily, your awareness of plonker driving helps you to anticipate this sort of thing. But WTF?'

One tagged in and asked TfL: 'Is this an acceptable behaviour of your bus driver?'

But opposition included a response: 'See how early you knew he was going to come over but you still don't anticipate it? You're being a a****** plain and simple.'

Another said: 'Why you riding in the middle of the lane like that?' 

Vine was also asked: 'Jeremy, with the amount of danger you're constantly involved in, instead of posting videos, why don't you simply take the bus and feel safe?'

Rosie Trew, Tfl's head of bus service delivery, told MailOnline: 'The footage of this incident is very concerning.

'We demand the highest standards of London’s bus drivers and we are working with the operator, RATP Dev Transit London, to carry out a thorough investigation.'

Vine, who has been described as 'willing to die in defence of the Highway Code', has caught dozens of people out during his regular commutes around the capital.  

His post this morning tagged in social media account of Will Norman, London's walking and cycling commissioner.

Mr Norman has been supporting the proposals for the Holland Park roundabout which he has labelled 'one of the most dangerous junctions on the TfL road network'.

He said last month: 'This scheme will make it safer for people walking and cycling and will help improve journey times for bus users without any significant impacts on general traffic, building a better, safer city for all Londoners.'

Public consultation on the measures ended last Sunday, with critics including Kensington's Conservative MP Felicity Buchan.

She said: 'I want to ensure the safety of cyclists, and I am supportive of measures to improve cycling safety but I do not believe this scheme gets that balance right.'

Concerns include that the cycle lane could cause extra congestion and air pollution, slow down bus and emergency vehicle journey times and displace traffic into neighbouring 'rat runs'.

Other films shared online by Vine from his cycling journeys have featured altercations with van drivers, with one telling him to 'f*** off' - while a London bus driver branded him a 'terrible person'.

He has also sparred with an Amazon driver who called him a 'p****' and a 4x4 driver he caught eating from a bag of chips and using her phone while at the wheel.

Vine recorded last September a near-miss with an HGV driver who cut across a cycle lane in central London without indicating - with the broadcaster adding the online caption: 'This is the guy who kills you.'

Vine has also attracted attention for using a novelty Penny Farthing bicycle whilst out and about. But he had a nasty fall from the Victorian-style velocipede in February 2022

He has also posted a mock apology video in response to an online backlash after an angry confrontation with a driver who failed to stop when the cycling presenter approached a junction in Hammersmith, west London.

But last November, Vine was left speechless for the first time during his recordings after he caught a van driver 'performing a sex act and watching porn' while driving through central London.

He told MailOnline at the time: 'This breaks some sort of record. I have no words.'

Sharing the footage, the 58-year-old later tweeted: 'Masturbating at the wheel of a vehicle while watching porn. This is extreme even for Cannon Street.'
