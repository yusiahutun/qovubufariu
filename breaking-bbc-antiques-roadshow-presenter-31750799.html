BBC Antiques Roadshow presenter Henry Sandon dies aged 95 on Christmas Day

Antiques Roadshow expert Henry Sandon has died at the age of 95, his family has confirmed.

The antiques expert died peacefully at a care home in Malvern, Worcestershire on Christmas Day morning, his son John said. John, who joined Antiques Roadshow like his father, described Henry as "a hugely popular television presenter and a giant in the world of antiques".

Henry got his big break on BBC One show Antiques Roadshow in 1979. He had been working as the curator of the Dyson Perrins Museum at the Royal Worcester factory in 1967.

In full, John said: "To the millions who tuned in every Sunday evening to watch The Antiques Roadshow, Henry was like a favourite uncle, whose enthusiasm for even the humblest piece of chipped china was infectious. His joy when he discovered a rare Staffordshire pottery owl jug, nicknamed 'Ozzie', was a magic TV moment few will forget."

The Museum of Royal Worcester also paid tribute in a touching statement. It read: "It is with great sadness we share the news that Henry Sandon passed away on Christmas morning.

"Our curator and then patron of the Museum for many years, a much-loved expert who shared his knowledge and enthusiasm for pots and Worcester in person, in books and on TV. Sorely missed."

Henry had been married to wife Barbara for 56 years, before she died in 2013. They had three sons – David, Peter and John.

Henry is survived too by three grandchildren and two great grandchildren. Many others have paid tribute to the television star too.

Auctioneer Phillip Serrell, who has appeared on the likes of Bargain Hunt and Flog It, praised Henry for doing "so much to promote antiques". And Henry certainly had an eye for them.

He started collection pottery after finding broken plates in his garden near Worcester's cathedral. Some of the pieces in his private collection dated as far back as 100BC.

Just this year, it was sold at auction. It is estimated to have made around £50,000.
