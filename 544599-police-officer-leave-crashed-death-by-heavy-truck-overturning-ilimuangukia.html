Police Officer on Leave Crushed to Death by Heavy Truck after Overturning: &quot;Ilimuangukia&quot;

  • A tragic crash in Turbo claimed the life of a Fort Tenan police officer who was on his annual leave
  • The cop was riding a motorcycle when a trailer rammed into him in the Mwamba area in Turbo, where it overturned
  • Four others were severely injured and were referred to the Moi Teaching and Referral Hosptial for specialised treatment

Kai Eli, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

A law enforcer attached to the Fort Tenan Police Station in Kericho died tragically after being hit by a heavy trailer on the morning of Thursday, April 11.

The heavy mover was on its way to Eldoret from Webuye when it rammed into two motorcycles in the Mwamba area of Turbo in Kakamega's Lugari sub-county.

How Kericho cop met death in Lugari

One of the riders was the officer who died on the spot as the driver of the heavy truck fled the accident scene.

Four others were severely injured at the scene and were rushed to the Turbo Sub-County Hospital before they were later referred to Eldoret's Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital.

Eyewitnesses confiding in NTV shared that the cop was riding in the same direction with the truck, which overturned and fell over him.

"We found the officer's body crushed after it fell over him. They were both headed in the right direction. There has been reckless driving on this road, and we would urge the government to install bumps because this is a residential area," said one of the witnesses.

Confirming the tragedy, Lugari Sub County Police Commander Bernard Ngungu revealed the deceased cop was on his annual leave.

How many accident deaths have been in Kenya in 2024?

In a recent report by the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA), at least 1,189 people died in road accidents since the beginning of the year 2024.

According to the authority, the number was higher by 60 deaths compared to the same period in 2023.

The NTSA data released on April 1 showed that 7,189 people in the country were involved in road traffic accidents.

This was 1,908 more than the number of accidents within the same period in 2023.

As per the NTSA 2023 data, at least 1,129 people died from road crashes.
