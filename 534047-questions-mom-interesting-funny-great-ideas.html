50 questions to ask your mom (interesting, funny, and great ideas)

You know your mom as a mother. She is loving, caring, and kind and would move the world for you. But are you familiar with her other side? Do you know about her childhood? Her favourites? Her dislikes? If you want to know your mother better, there are games and questions to ask your mom. You may realize that you only know a tiny part of your mother as an individual once she answers the questions.

If you know your mother well enough, you will know which questions to pose and which you cannot. Do not go overboard with the questions; go for light and easygoing queries. If you are unsure how to go about the whole thing, start with favourites.

If you run out of questions, you can always try questions to ask your mom to see if she knows you. This will give you a break to think of more questions and help your mother understand you better. Get to play this game of questions while you enjoy an activity together, like cooking or hiking.

Best questions to ask your mom

Questions to ask your mom about her childhood make an easy start. Ask about her favourite childhood snacks or what she misses most about growing up. Gradually move to deep questions that will tell you about her personality.

Deep questions to ask your mom

Deep questions are an excellent way of learning your mother’s mindset. You will understand her general being and why she approaches life the way she does. Wondering why your mom does not tolerate some habits? Ask her the following deep questions:

  • Tell me one thing that scared you as a kid and a backstory. Are you still scared?
  • Can you remember that one family trip that made you appreciate family?
  • What are some peculiar habits or talents that father has that you think none of my siblings know?
  • Do we have family heirlooms with an unexpected or funny story behind them?
  • Is there an uncle or aunt who is always hilarious, no matter the situation?
  • What’s the one thing you have from either your mother or grandmother? And are you going to pass it down to any of us?
  • What’s our family’s unique or unusual tradition? Can you share how it started?
  • Can you share a time when an uncle or aunt did something completely unexpected at a family get-together?
  • Which relative do I look alike? Do we share any character that I do not know of?
  • What was the most embarrassing moment for you or your family during a family function?
  • Is there a funny misunderstanding that’s become a classic family tale?
  • Would you rather hike as a family or rent a movie theatre with us?
  • Who was your favourite pop musician during your younger years? Have you ever gone to their concert?

Hypothetical questions to ask your mom

Hypothetical questions are not real and, more often than not, will never occur. Try asking hypothetical questions and include questions to ask your mother about yourself if you want to make the moment more fun. Here are made-up questions that your mom will answer freely:

  • What if our family were to take part in one reality TV show? Which family do you think is closer to how we live?
  • Imagine you’ve just won an Oscar. Which movie and which role are you being awarded for?
  • If you could have a superpower for a day, what would it be, and how would you use it?
  • What is that one celebrity you wish could come back to life? Do you ever think of their passing, and if you do, how often?
  • What is that one topic you can lecture on without doing any research?
  • If you had to live in a movie universe for a month, which one would you pick?
  • Dogs or cats? If either could speak a human language, which one would you spend your days talking to?
  • Imagine that we are being transported to a different universe. What is that one thing you would ensure you carry in your purse?
  • If you were allowed to rename our street, what name would you choose?

Funny questions to ask your mom

Do you want to know your mum's childhood crush? Or the time she and her friends got in trouble? A childhood secret she has been keeping from everyone? Go with the funny questions. Ask your mum the following funny questions to know her perception of everyday life:

  • Why did you choose to give me the name that I use now?
  • How much was your allowance, and how did you choose to spend it?
  • What is your favourite kind of cake, and would you eat it every day if you had the ability to?
  • As a kid, what did you think you wanted to be when you grew up?
  • Did you have a nickname growing up? Is it something you would want people to call you right now?
  • Do you remember the name of your crush in high school?
  • What were your most and least favourite subjects in school?
