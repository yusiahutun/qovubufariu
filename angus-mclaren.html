Early Life and Education

Angus Mclaren was born on November 3, 1988, in Wonthaggi, Victoria, Australia. His age is currently 34 years old. He was born to his father Peter McLaren and his mother Kerena McLaren.

He was raised on a dairy farm near Loch along with his two siblings, Rhett McLaren, and Aidan McLaren. Likewise, he holds Australian nationality and is of a white ethnic background. According to his date of birth, he was born under the sun sign Scorpio.

As for his academic history, he is a graduate student. He completed his graduation from Mary Mackillop College Leongatha. He then applied to the Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts (WAAPA), located in Perth, Western Australia, and enrolled in their acting bachelor’s degree program.

He was the drummer for the band Rapids (Ballet Imperial) and the bass player for the group Bogey Lowensteins.

Career and Professional Life

2000-2013

He made several appearances in unprofessional and school productions for the Leongatha Lyric Theatre. When he was 12 years old, he made his professional debut in a recurring role on the ABC series Something in the Air. Additional TV appearances followed, including guest spots on Blue Heelers, Comedy Inc., and a recurring role on Neighbours. Other series appearances included the kids’ shows Worst Best Friends, The Saddle Club, and Fergus McPhail.

With the kid’s show Silversun, which aired on both the Seven Network and ABC, McLaren received his first main cast part in 2004. He then made his debut in a major motion picture in 2005 with Rohan Michael Hoole’s low-budget Melbourne film Court of Lonely Royals. McLaren also contributed to Last Man Standing in 2005, and he established himself as a reliable voice actor for commercials.

The children’s television program H2O: Just Add Water hired McLaren in 2006, and he played Lewis McCartney in seasons one through three, from the show’s debut to midseason. His Nathan Rafter character first appeared in Packed to the Rafters in 2008. Throughout the program’s six seasons, he appeared in 102 episodes.

2013-Present

He attended a variety of master classes in New York, LA, Sydney, and Melbourne throughout 2013 and into 2014. At the Western Australian Academy of Performing Arts in Perth, Western Australia, he later pursued a Bachelor of Acting degree.

Angus participated in eight stage productions at WAAPA while he was there. He played The Marquis De Sade in the WAAPA production of Marat/Sade, The Man in Tender Napalm, and in the year before he graduated, he received rave reviews for his performance as Coriolanus, the play’s title character.

He portrayed William “The Crimson Fiddler” Graham in Quartermaine in 2016. For his portrayal of Will in this short film, he took home the Nine Network Channel 9 Best Actor Award at the WA Screen Academy Awards. During the thirty-first season of the Seven Network soap opera Home and Away, it was revealed in 2018 that McLaren had joined the cast as the recurring character Lance Salisbury. He made his debut in episode 6860, which aired on April 17, 2018.
