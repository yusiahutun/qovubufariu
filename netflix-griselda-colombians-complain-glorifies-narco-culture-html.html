The creators of Narcos have topped Netflix charts once again with their latest limited series, Griselda.

The six-part show follows Griselda Blanco, played by Sofia Vergara, 51, on her journey from Medellin in Colombia to becoming the 'Godmother' of Miami's raging drug scene.

Inspired by real-life events, the dramatization blends facts and fiction as it documents Griselda's reign of terror during the 1970s and '80s where she became a billionaire.

Her rise to the top of the drugs trade began in early life when she reportedly was caught up in crime from a young age

It was claimed she was caught up in prostitution and pickpocketing as a child - and it has even been reported she was involved in the kidnapping of a young boy when she was 11 years old.

Sofia Vergara (pictured), 51, transformed into drug 'queenpin' Griselda Blanco for her latest project

An unverified story claims she took it upon herself to shoot the boy dead after his wealthy parents refused to pay the ransom.

In her personal life, Griselda married a small-time criminal when she was still a teenager - and the pair went on to have three children.

When the couple divorced, Griselda was alleged to have ordered her ex-husband's murder. 

But she went on to find love again with drug trafficker Alberto Bravo - and from here, she began to climb the ladder in the cocaine industry. 

Nicknamed the 'godmother of cocaine', she was earning $80 million a month at the height of her empire, and went to extreme lengths to ensure any hint of competition was eradicated.

The baron was thought to be responsible for around 200 deaths of rivals - usually carried out in broad daylight by men on motorcycles.

Reflecting on the case in 1993, The Drug Enforcement Administration said: 'Griselda loved killings. Bodies lined the streets of Miami as a result of her feuds.

'She gathered around her a group of henchmen known as the Pistoleros. Initiation into the group was earned by killing someone and cutting off a body part as proof of the deed...

'She not only killed rivals and wayward lovers, but used murder as a means of cancelling debts she didn't want to pay. A particularly bloody massacre that took place in July 1979 in a Miami shopping center became known as the Dadeland Massacre.'

As she raked in the cash through her criminal activity, Griselda taunted authorities hy spending conspicuously - reportedly splashing out on luxury properties and hedonistic parties.

She also brazenly named her son Michael Corleone - after the crime boss in The Godfather film series with the first installment released in 1972. 

But the drug 'queenpin' couldn't have succeeded without the help of close allies and  business associates, while June Hawkins-Singleton and Raul Diaz were both real police officers who united to end Griselda Blanco's reign of terror in the 1980s. 

But where are those who witnessed Griselda Blanco's rise to the top today? Here, FEMAIL reveals where the pioneers of Miami's drug empire wound up... 

Griselda Blanco 

Griselda Blanco, pictured left, was a barely 5ft tall Colombian 'queenpin' linked to 250 murders while she ran her billion dollar empire. Actress Sofia in character as Griselda - pictured right

As depicted in the Netflix series, business began to take a downturn after Griselda was found guilty of drugs charges in 1985 and incarcerated.

She received the maximum sentence of 15 years but was still able to run her empire from behind bars.

However, the conviction spurred authorities to attempt to press additional charges against the career criminal - including more than 200 murders.

By 1994, one of her former hitmen - Jorge Ayala - agreed to testify against her and Griselda was charged with three murders.

One of them was that of a two-year-old boy who was fatally shot during a failed assassination attempt of his father - who was one of Griselda's former enforcers.

Griselda married multiple times and produced four children, the youngest of whom she named - in a flash of dark humor - Michael Corleone Blanco

The investigation stalled but the drug baron decided to plead guilty in 1998 in a bid to reduce her sentence.

Following her release after 10 years in prison, she was deported to Colombia where she is said to have 'retired' from her life of crime.

But it appeared her past caught up with her and, in 2012, Griselda was shot and killed as she stepped out of a butcher shop in Medellin, aged 69.

She still ranks among the most notorious drug lords in history and has been a central figure in documentaries such Cocaine Cowboys (2006) and Cocaine Cowboys 2: Hustlin' with the Godmother (2008) - as well as film The Cocaine Godmother (2017) starring Catherine Zeta Jones.

Griselda's sons 

Since their mother's incarceration, Dixon, Osvaldo and Uber have all been murdered.

The last living son of Griselda Blanco is Michael Corleone Blanco, 41, who by his teens, was largely in control of the family business.

By the time he was an adult the majority of his family were either deceased or incarcerated, leading him to largely be raised by legal guardians and his maternal grandmother.

In 2008, he told AllHipHop: 'I would meet someone that my mother would know.'

'And I would say, 'I'm going to live in your house and I'm going to pay you rent and you're going to be my legal guardian.''

The mother-of-four would often state throughout the Netflix show that her son's are the inspiration for her drug empire 

Michael landed himself under house arrest in 2012 on one count of conspiracy to traffic cocaine and two felony counts of cocaine, The Miami New Times reports.

Jorge 'Rivi' Ayala-Rivera 

Rivi, played by Martin Rodriguez, was a loyal hitman to Griselda.

The elusive killer was responsible for some of the most shocking lines in the series, from a sex phone scandal to the death of Jesus 'Chuco' Castro's two-year-old son and robbing a bank for quarters.

And the plotlines largely align with reality.

Rivi was caught by police in 1993 and was convicted of three murders. During this time, Rivi tactically became embroiled in a sex phone scandal.

Jorge 'Rivi' Ayala-Rivera (depicted in the Netflix show on the left, and in real life on the right), was Griselda's hitman 

Doug Miro explained in Tudum that Rivi, now 66, was involved in a sex phone scandal when serving time for three murders.

June Hawkins-Singleton and Raul Diaz

June Hawkins-Singleton and Raul Diaz were both real police officers who united to end Griselda Blanco's reign of terror in the 1980s.

The pair became an integral part of the anti-drug task force dubbed CENTAC- Central Tactical Unit.

June, now 73, lives in Nashville with her husband Al Singleton - the same man who cracked the case with her. She retired in 2004, according to Radio Times.

The former police officer worked as a series consultant on the show and helped Juliana Aiden Martinez, who plays June in the series, by chatting through her memories of that time and how to be best depicted.

In conversation with Tudum, she confirmed that the show's portrayal of harassment instances were not inaccurate, especially since she was one of the first female officers in the Miami area.

June Hawkins-Singleton (pictured), now 73, currently lives in Nashville with her husband Al Singleton 

Played by Juliana Aiden Martinez (pictured), June played a key part in running the CENTAC unit in Miami 

The series portrays a scene where June is subjected to bullying from male members of staff by turning the air conditioning up to observe her hardened nipples, an instance 'you just rolled with,' she explained.

In 2017, June was featured in an episode of the Law Enforcement Talk podcast and discussed her career.

She said: 'Working in homicide during '79, '80, '81 is probably not only the thing I'm most proud of, but the thing I most enjoyed because I really did impact... it was exciting, it was purposeful, I had a fire in the belly and lightning in my veins, and I was going to get those bad guys.'

Does Netflix's Griselda glorify Narco culture?

Social media users have complained that the series glorifies Griselda's violent history.

Taking to TikTok, a Colombian content creator called Velu, said: 'Me watching Griselda after reading the true story and the horrible things this woman did and knowing that a lot of people are going to glorify her story.

Elsewhere, Velu, from Colombia, said that despite Sofia Vergara's impressive performance, there are 'better stories to tell'

'Real people died because of her, and she doesn't represent Colombian values. Colombians are much more than this,' she concluded.

Velu summarized her thoughts in the caption: 'Sofia Vergara did a tremendous job in her performance. But we have better stories to tell.'

Elsewhere, Kevin Figman, from Columbia, also took to TikTok to express similar thoughts.

Colombian Kevin Figman said Netflix's series will encourage a negative stereotype of his country 

He said: 'Unfortunately, this is a reputation and a stereotype we have to live with nowadays, Colombia always being related to Narcos and drugs, even though Colombia isn't really like that anymore.'

'The city of Medellin is now one of the safest and secure cities in all Latin America,' he added.

Kevin called for a more diverse range of films covering Latin America because 'Encanto was the only movie we got that wasn't about drugs'.

'If we keep consuming these kinds of things, they're going to keep making them,' he concluded.

Darío Sepúlveda 

In the show, Dario, played by Alberto Guerra, is depicted as Griselda's loyal lover, partner in crime, and father of her fourth child, Michael.

The pair enjoy an all-encompassing romance in the series until Griselda's hubris runs wild.

Dario is forced to 'save' Michael and escaped to Colombia following a birthday party where a crack-fuelled Griselda forces a pair to have sex at gunpoint.

While some details of their romance may be speculated over in the show, Griselda and Dario did wed in 1978.

Fabio and Jorge Ochoa 

Griselda is not the first time the Ochua brothers have landed themselves in a Netflix series, as the siblings were previously depicted in Narcos.

Unsurprisingly, the pair had a significant role in bringing about the genesis of the Colombian cartel.

In the series, Fabio and Jorge are portrayed as Griselda's trusted business associates and the reason she eventually flees Miami.

The series shows Griselda with Fabio and Jorge's cousin, Marta, who accidentally overdoses in her presence.

Jorge Ochua (pictured in the Netflix series) is thought to have amassed $6 billion from the lucrative drug industry with his brothers 

Jorge Luis Ochoa (pictured) served a short prison sentence and currently lives in Colombia (he is pictured in 1988)

Fabio Ochoa Vasquez was captured along with another 29 narco-smugglers by Colombian authorities in 1999 (pictured). His current location is unknown 

Worried that her death would appear like a murder, Griselda escaped to California to start life fresh once again.

In real life, Marta was not a cousin of Fabio and Jorje but a sister, and the cause of her death is not known for certain.

However, the series did accurately represent the wealth amassed by the brothers.

Business Insider estimated that they made approximately $6 billion from the drug trade. They were also included in Forbes first World's Billionaires List in 1987. 

By 1991, the Ochoa brothers all turned themselves in to the police for drug-related charges, according to Esquire.

Jorge was released in 1996 after serving a five-and-a-half-year sentence, and he is living in Colombia.

Meanwhile, Fabio was arrested in Bogota in 1999. He was captured along with another 29 narco-smugglers by Colombian authorities as part of an international sting operation code-named 'Operation Millennium' involving the US, Mexico, and Ecuador. 

It is thought that he served a short sentece, but his whereabouts are unknown.
