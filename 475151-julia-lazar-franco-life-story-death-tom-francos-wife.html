Who was Julia Lazar Franco?

Julia Lazar is best known as the lead singer of the rock band Exit and the wife of American actor Tom Franco. Additionally, she gained fame as a camera operator, publicist, and manager that took care of the Franco brothers' business.

Julia was born on November 7, 1957, in Vienna, Austria, and is of Hungarian and Austrian descent. She is the daughter of Imre Lazar, a movie producer, and Marika Nagy, a painting artist. Julia spent her childhood in Vienna.

Lazar inherited her mother's artistic talent. She moved to the United States of America and joined the Californian artist community in the early 1980s.

After completing her formal education, Julia met her first love, Richard Schoenherz, a music composer and musician. She created the rock band Exit and became the lead singer. The band gained fame in the area.

In the early 90s, Julia left for India for soul-searching and in search of spirituality. She settled in the city of Ganeshpuri, and during this time, she learnt yoga and resided in an ashram that is a reserved religious monastery.

She left India for Paris, where she enrolled at the Le Cordon Bleu Culinary Institute. While there, she gained remarkable skills in cooking and developed her passion for the same.

Julia returned to the United States of America in the early 2000s and settled in Bay Area, California. She again aligned herself with another ashram in Oakland where Tom Franco was initially managing.

Who is Julia Franco’s husband, Tom Franco?

Tom Franco is an American actor, artist, and the founder of the Firehouse Art Collective in Berkeley, California. He is the brother of renowned actors James Franco and Dave Franco.

He started his acting career in the 1990s and made his debut in the movie Basket, a horror comedy movie. He has appeared in other productions, including The Devil Wears a Toupee (2007), The Disaster Artist (2017), and A Piece of History (2005), to mention but a few.

How did Julia Lazar Franco die?

Julia Lazar Franco had been suffering from various health complications that eventually led to her death in August 2014. She passed on at 57 from complications from kidney failure and Hepatitis C.

A memorial service was held in her honour on August 31, 2014, and was attended by a large crowd. Julia's husband later married Iris Torres, a Mexican-born movie producer, in 2015.
