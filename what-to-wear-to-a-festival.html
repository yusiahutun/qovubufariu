Festival Fashion Guide 2023: What to wear to a festival if you're going to Glastonbury, Wilderness,

Dust off your dancing boots because festival season is finally here. Glastonbury may be over but Wilderness, All Points East, Field Day, Boardmasters, Lost Village and many more are still incoming.

You probably know the drill when it comes to British festival fashion - a waterproof and comfortable shoes always make the dress code, but otherwise, there's no better place to wear that outfit you were dreaming of all winter.

With high street and designer brands offering styles that are just asking to be worn for a hedonistic, mud-filled weekend, we've found all of the best pieces from ASOS to Free People and Urban Outfitters to & Other Stories. Scroll on to the shop the edit, plus a shopping list of essentials.

Festival essentials

The biggest festival fashion trends at Glastonbury 2023

The most viral outfit from this year's Glastonbury goes to Sienna Miller. The fairly simple ensemble of double denim and white cowboy boots was exactly what we'd expect from one of fashion's longest serving It-girls: effortless. Her jacket and shorts are from Self-Portrait while you can shop similar boots all over the high street.

We weren't surprised to see crochet dominate at Glastonbury - it's one of this season's biggest trends after all. Poppy Delevingne looked gorgeous in her striped crochet mini dress by Nobody's Child and it's still available to shop on the website in limited sizes (plus lots of similar styles). Just add wellies or chunky boots.

Wax jackets are forever a festival staple and none are more iconic than Barbour's. From Alexa Chung's longstanding penchant for the British brand to both Gemma Chan and Lily James stepping out in them this year, they're a failsafe fashion piece. Shop Gemma's Barbour Eddleston zip wax jacket on sale now at Very.

Festival fashion dos and don'ts I've learnt over the years

Do: Take layers. The British weather can change very quickly, so anything you can chuck on and off quickly depending on the sun and rain will be a lifesaver. Think mini dresses, lightweight jumpers and rain jackets.

Don't: Buy a brand new pair of trainers for the occasion. If you're lucky enough to avoid the mud and rain, they'll probably still get ruined in a dust bath as soon as you go near a stage. Bring an old pair you don't care about or stick to boots. And it should go without saying, but you'll definitely regret wearing heels.

Do: Mix high street pieces with vintage finds. If you've ever considered thrifting, a festival is the perfect opportunity to start. A vintage fringed jacket worn with your new Mango mini will make sure you're in an outfit no one else is wearing.

Don't: Forget your sunglasses. Not just ideal for saving your eyes when you want to watch your favourite band in the blaring sun, they'll also hide a multitude of sins the next morning (ditto to face glitter).

How to pack your clothes for a festival

A rucksack you can both wear on your back or pull on wheels depending where you are is ideal for a festival, and will take you from the city to the muddy fields as painlessly as possible. Consider investing in packing cubes so you can pack all of your outfits (including accessories) separately, which will make your life so much easier when you're (possibly hungover) putting together your outfits in the morning.

When it comes to toiletries, makeup and haircare, a hard case like Antler's Clifton Vanity is a game-changer. It's robust but lightweight and comes with a detachable strap.
