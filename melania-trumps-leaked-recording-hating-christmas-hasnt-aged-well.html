Melania Trump's leaked recording 'hating' Christmas hasn't aged well

Melania Trump wasn’t a fan of Christmas one year, and netizens have sworn to bring back the leaked audio footage of the former First Lady complaining every single year.

There is no denying the saga around Melania Trump’s White House decorations creeps back this time every year. It became the Internet’s obsession at the time. From the blood-red spooky tress in 2018 to the snow white vibe in 2017, she embraced her role of performing a Christmas makeover of the White House. The cherry on top was her final Christmas decorations in 2020 with the theme, “America the Beautiful” which featured a tree filled with ornaments dedicated to children around the country. While the 53-year-old celebrity may not love Christmas as much as her followers, she definitely managed to make the decorations intriguing each year.

Melania Trump’s leaked audio about Christmas is living on the Internet

The former fashion model’s infamous rant about Christmas has been resurfacing on X (formerly Twitter) almost every yea. It was first leaked in 2020 on CNN’s Anderson Cooper 360 show.

Melania was allegedly secretly recorded in the summer of 2018 where she shared her frustration with the staff.

“I’m working … my a** off on the Christmas stuff, that you know, who gives a f*** about the Christmas stuff and decorations? But I need to do it, right?,” the former first lady said on the audio.

She added: “OK, and then I do it and I say that I’m working on Christmas and planning for the Christmas, and they said, ‘Oh, what about the children that they were separated?’ Give me a f****** break.”

Melania Trump does actually like decorating for Christmas

Gone are the days when Melania hated Christmas like other celebrities, 2023 proved to be a fresh start for the former First Lady who launched a special set of NFTs for the holiday season.

She announced: “I am pleased to continue USA Memorabilia’s tradition of celebrating the season with A Red, White and Blue Christmas. This year, I found myself looking to my love for our great nation for inspiration. May everyone experience an abundance of peace & love during the holiday season.”

The virtual Christmas ornaments are listed at $35 each on the website, where followers can choose from over five designs.
