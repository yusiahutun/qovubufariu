How to report M-Pesa fraudsters and conmen in Kenya easily

The widespread adoption of M-PESA as a payment system in Kenya has made it an attractive target for criminals. Fraudsters exploit the system and steal from Kenyans. Luckily, Safaricom offers an effective way to report M-Pesa fraudsters to prevent theft.

Fraudsters and conmen use different strategies to steal from M-PESA users. For example, some fraudsters target your M-PESA account balance through ATMs, messages, and USSD. They may also trick you into providing personal information or financial details.

Once they obtain the information, they steal money from your M-PESA account. You must be aware of these strategies and take steps to protect your accounts. But how can you report M-PESA fraudsters and recover your money?

How to report M-PESA fraudsters and conmen in Kenya

Many people have fallen victim to different strategies used by conmen and fraudsters to defraud or steal from unsuspecting M-PESA users. If you find yourself in this situation, you can report a scammer by doing the following.

Contact Safaricom Customer Care

The quickest way to report M-PESA fraudsters is to contact Safaricom customer services. You can call the toll-free number at 100 for prepay or 200 for PostPay customers (free of charge). Safaricom also receives calls through the number 234 for M-PESA-related queries. Alternatively, you can call +254-722-002-100 (chargeable).

Secondly, you can send an email to customercare@safaricom.co.ke. And if you use social media accounts, you can contact Safaricom using the following handles:

When contacting Safaricom via social media, you may be required to provide your phone number or the affected number.

Forward the message to 333

In most cases, fraudsters use hoax messages to steal from unsuspecting users. Hoax SMS comes in many forms. For example, you may receive a fake promotional SMS telling you to claim your prize. Or, you may receive a phony relief payment SMS, fake loans, or fake recipient.

Once you receive hoax messages, forward them to 333 for free. The number 333 is the Safaricom number for reporting fraudsters or reporting hoax messages. When you forward a message to 333, Safaricom's security team will investigate the message to determine if it is from a legitimate source, scam, or phishing attempt.

If the message is a scam, Safaricom will act appropriately to protect other customers, including blocking the sender. However, if the SMS is from a legitimate source, Safaricom will not take further action or will provide you with the necessary information and resources.

Report to the police

Sometimes, some frauds are too large for Safaricom to handle. In such instances, it becomes helpful to report an M-PESA agent, fraudster, or conman to the police. The police will investigate and take action against the perpetrators. You must visit your local police station to report the matter.

You can also contact the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) and report the fraud. Part of DCI’s work is to receive and investigate fraud complaints from commercial banks, financial institutions, and government parastatals. Use the numbers 020-334-3312, 020-720-2000, or the toll-free number 0800-722-203 to call the DCI.

How to recover money from an M-PESA scammer

It can be challenging to recover money from a scammer in Kenya. However, there are steps you can take to get your money back.

Initiate an M-PESA reversal

You can request a reversal by sending an SMS to 456. Here is what you should do:

  • After completing the transaction, send the whole message to 456.
  • You will receive a response from Reversal.
  • Your reversal request will initiate.
  • After the process begins, you will receive an update within two hours.

You can also initiate a reversal request by SMSing 2530 with the transaction number. Alternatively, use USSD Code 234 to Reverse, dial *234# on your Till Line to request a reversal, or call Safaricom Customer Care on 234 again with the reversal request.

Request for an M-PESA reversal on the mySafaricom APP

Use the mySafaricom app to request a reversal if you sent money to a scammer. However, this method only works if you did the transaction using the mySafaricom app. Follow the steps below:

  • After completing the transaction, tap on the Reverse transaction icon.
  • Confirm if you want to raise a reversal request.
  • Safaricom will receive your request for processing and feedback.
  • You should receive feedback within two minutes.
  • You will then receive a message from Reversal updating you on the reversal request.

At this stage, Safaricom will do a Follow-up with the recipient will. Finally, you will get feedback within two hours. If the reversal is successful, you will receive your money in your M-PESA account.

Talk to the police

Generally, it is best to report the scam to the police. The police will investigate and file charges against scammers or fraudsters. If you are lucky and your case goes to court, the court may request the con artist to repay your money.

How to protect your M-PESA account

While Safaricom and M-PESA give you many ways of reporting fraudsters, the best strategy is to protect yourself to avoid losing money. Here are several things you can do to protect your M-PESA account.

  • Prevent SIM Swap by dialling *100*100#.
  • If someone tries registering a new line using your national ID number, Safaricom will send you an SMS to confirm whether you are registering a new line. You will receive an SMS from number 707, and it is best to verify the message.
  • Recognise the official Safaricom contacts. Safaricom will only call you from the number 0722000000.
  • Even if Safaricom or M-PESA staff calls you, do not give them your M-PESA PIN, SIM passcodes, or passwords. Safaricom staff will never ask you to share personal information.
  • Avoid sending money to unknown callers.
  • Avoid sharing personal details with anyone on Safaricom branded links, websites, or social media platforms.
  • Avoid pressing any codes on your phone as directed by anyone purporting to be from Safaricom. The codes could block your line or send money to a conman.
  • Always protect your personal information, such as your national ID number, date of birth, and address, because fraudsters can use them to steal from your M-PESA account.
  • Avoid callers requesting you to offer refunds to M-PESA transactions. Instead, advise them to use 456 or call Safaricom support services to reverse their money.
  • Register your voice as your password.
  • Be wary of promotional or hoax messages from unknown senders.
  • You can avoid fraudulent activities by ensuring your SIM card has an active SIM lock. In other words, use strong passwords and keep personal information off social media.
  • Avoid callers who claim your number is double registered. Double registration of phone numbers is impossible.
