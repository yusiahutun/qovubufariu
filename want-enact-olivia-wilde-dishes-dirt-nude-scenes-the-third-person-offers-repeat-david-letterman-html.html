She stripped bare for her latest movie, Third Person.

And as Olivia Wilde dished the dirt on how awkward it was to run down a set of stairs completely in the nude, she jokingly offered to 're-enact' it for David Letterman.

The 30-year-old actress was a guest on The Late Show on Wednesday, where she discussed what it was like to look back on her pre-baby body on film.

Olivia rocked a very eclectic look to appear on the New York City based talk show.

She teamed an electric blue skirt decorated with tiny quarter moons with a black and yellow bumble-bee inspired T-shirt.

The House actress wore her long brunette tresses out and styled in elegant waves around her shoulders as she enjoyed an animated chat with the famed host.

'Do you want me to re-enact it?' The 30-year-old actress almost gave Letterman a show when he kept probing for information on the nude shoot

There she is! Olivia Wilde goes nude for scenes in Third Person alongside Liam Neeson

Letterman wasted no time in raising the issue of her nude scenes for her new movie Third Person, a romantic drama exploring three intertwining love stories.

Olivia took the opportunity to reminisce on what things were like before she and husband Jason Sudeikis welcomed their first son Otis on April 20.

'It's great now that I've had a baby because I can look at it and be like, 'yep, that was once the way it was,'' the star quipped about her naked body in the movie.

Got him: The pretty actress and new mother held her own when Letterman tried to ask for details about the production of the nude scenes

'But its funny because we shot it in Italy,' the star went on, 'and for them being naked in no big deal.'

When Letterman pushed further to find out exactly 'how' it was that Olivia executed those naked scenes, she came back and held her own.

'Do you want me to re-enact it?' she asked, pretending to stand up to get into position.

Letterman withdrew.

 

'I noticed that it had been strategically removed from the film': Olivia didn't mind that the parts where she ran down stairs naked were cut

Although from an audience's perspective it may not look like much, Olivia revealed that she had to go over the nude scenes multiple times in order to get them right during production.

To add insult to injury, the scenes she had to repeat included a deleted scene where she runs down a flight of stairs.

'Not only did I have to do it again and again and again, but this was a scene in which I was running down stairs naked,' the actress lamented, adding: 'which no-one should ever do on camera.'

In what she apparently considers a stroke of luck, those scenes never made the final cut.

New mother: Olivia and her husband Jason Sudeikis have recently welcomed their first son, Otis

No big deal: The confident star spoke about her nude scenes with no nervousness

'I noticed that it had been strategically removed from the film, which I approve of!'

Continuing on the topic of running down stairs naked, Olivia expressed her distaste for the activity.

'It's bad,' she told Letterman. 'There are things that no-one should see.'

Olivia graphically illustrated her point as follows: 'The body does things that it's not supposed to! It's jiggling! It's moving!

Third Person also stars Liam Neeson, Mila Kunis, Adrien Brody, James Franco and Kim Basinger, and is set for release on June 20 in the United States.
