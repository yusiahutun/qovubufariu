What is a Secondary School Leaving Certificate?

As the name suggests, the Kenya Secondary School Leaving Certificate is offered to students after completing their studies at the secondary school level. This is after completing the relevant examinations provided by the Kenya National Examinations Council for students at the secondary school level.

The certificate is proof that the student attended secondary school and left after having completed their studies. Also, it contains a section for the headteacher to report on the student's ability, which is one of the essential sections.

How to get a Secondary School Leaving Certificate

You can access your leaving certificate in Kenya by visiting your secondary school. The individual processing your request at the learning institution will ask you to tell your academic details. This usually includes your admission number and any other relevant information the school might require.

The school's representative will then check if you have any school fees balance and issue you with a school leaving certificate signed by the relevant authorities. Most schools don't charge any fees.

Can you get your school leaving certificate online?

Since the Kenya Secondary School Leaving certificate requires an official signature and stamp from the institution you attended, likely, you cannot get a dully-filled one online.

However, you can access and download the blank document from various sources online. But keep in mind that the school leaving certificate must be registered with the Ministry of Education under your respective learning institution.

The Kenya Secondary School Leaving Certificate is an essential document as it states that one completed their secondary education at their respective institution. Also, it informs on other matters other than grades like behaviour and co-curricular activities. Therefore, ascertain that you follow the steps mentioned earlier to access yours when required.
