GMB's Kate Garraway fumes people 'don't understand' as she's trolled over Derek doc

Kate Garraway has hit back after receiving criticism over her new documentary about her late husband, Derek Draper.

Derek died earlier this year, leaving behind his wife Kate and their children Darcey and Billy. Kate's new documentary, called Kate Garraway: Derek's Story, documents his final year of life.

Kate appeared on Good Morning Britain to discuss her reasons for doing the documentary and to emphasise its importance.

Susanna read a letter from a viewer called Pip, who understood why Kate wanted to tell Derek's story and highlighted that their husband's final wish was to have the story of his fight with cancer, his family's care for him and the plight of his carers told to everyone.

Kate responded to the backlash over her documentary, saying: "I've had a few comments on Twitter saying, 'oh for goodness sake, what now? ' and I knew that would happen because people don't understand and they probably haven't been through that situation maybe that Derek has experienced and millions of others do.", reports the Mirror.

Kate Garraway shared her feelings, saying: "I hope that people understand why. It is important not to just walk away from something just because the person for whom made it very relevant to our family life isn't there. It wouldn't be right for Derek. I hope people understand and I hope they find it helpful. There are moments of joy in there too as there always were along the way. Hopefully it'll be good."

Ed Balls mentioned that viewers might be upset to hear Kate was getting trolled. She answered: "I get a huge amount of love so it's fine to get a little bit of hate along the way as well because people can express their opinions. I just knew some people would say that but for the millions of people it would help, and Derek and the children, it felt the best thing to do."

Later, Kate said that Derek had wanted a third episode of their documentary because he "wanted to be heard".

Derek expressed his desire to be part of the film, saying: "I want to be heard,". Kate also shared her thoughts: "Obviously when we started making this documentary early last year, we had no idea the events that would unfold that ultimately took Derek from us.

"And in January 2024, I wondered if it was right that it should ever come to air. But I didn't want to let those who have given us so much support over the last four years down, and the carers paid and unpaid who in their thousands of letters to me, feel Derek's story has given them a voice. Also, I remembered so vividly that the idea of making this third documentary at all came from Derek himself."
