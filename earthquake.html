Career

He is an American stand-up comedian, actor, voice artist, and radio personality known as “Earthquake.” He currently hosts the SiriusXM and Kevin Hart’s Laugh Out Loud Network radio show Quakeshouse.

Earthquake performed a half-hour HBO special as part of the One Night Stand series, which premiered on August 26, 2005. His comedy specials are coveted fan collectibles and the stuff of legends. His film credits include a minor role in Kevin Smith’s cult classic Clerks II (2006) and a supporting role in The Longshots (2008). He has appeared on numerous television shows, including a recurring role on the CBS sitcom: The Neighborhood (2018-2021), starring Cedric The Entertainer, as well as appearances on South Side (2019), In the Cut (2017), and other shows. Earthquake’s other television appearances include a recurring role on Everybody Hates Chris as Chris’ Uncle Mike. He played Root the Rooster in the 2006 CGI film Barnyard as well as the video game of the same name, and he continues to do so in the TV series spinoff Back at the Barnyard.
