Rocky Horror's Nell Campbell admits on-set romps: 'I wouldn't call it romance'

So it's no surprise that there was a bit of hanky panky going on when the cameras had stopped rolling.

The cult classic's stars Nell Campbell and Patricia Quinn admitted that cast members would get intimate to kill time in between scenes – but wouldn't reveal who.

The actresses were asked by Rufus Hound at the Rocky Horror 40th anniversary screening whether there were any romances on set, to which Nell replied: "I'm not sure romance is the right word."

The 62-year-old, who played Columbia in the 1975 movie, said: "I don't frankly think I can talk about that.

"Well, we had to do something to kill time between shots."

Nell and Patricia answered questions about Rocky Horror at the anniversary event at Royal Albert Hall – although some attendees were more enamoured by their ah-may-zing outfits.

Patricia, who played domestic Magenta, joked that the biggest thing that she's taken from the experience was "money", while when asked what the weirdest thing she's done with a Rocky Horror fan, Nell responded: "Does that include sex?"

The stars also addressed at what age kids should be allowed watch Rocky Horror – and they're not a big fan of parental guidance.

Nell said: "I think kids should be watching it from the get-go. It amazes me when parents think, 'not until they're 13'. Are you kidding me? Pour them a drink and put on the DVD."

We want a night in at Nell's house.
