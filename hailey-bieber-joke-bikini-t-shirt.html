Hailey Bieber's bikini t-shirt is the most unexpected glam room must-have see photos

Anything worn by Hailey Bieber is almost guaranteed to spark a reaction. Revered for her forward-thinking fashion choices, there is a reason that the supermodel is the object of style inspiration for so many. This month alone the 26-year-old has rocked some truly major looks that we can't wait to recreate.

Between serving 'Old money' in a luscious £12k Saint Laurent faux fur, rocking an ultra-broad chocolate brown tailoring piece on her coffee run, and reviving the retro fleece, the 26-year-old has set the winter dressing bar sky high.

MORE: Hailey Bieber courtside style nails wearing three vintage trends at once

True to form, Hailey came through with another look that totally stopped us in our tracks, though we can't say for sure that we will be trying this one out anytime soon because of its distinctly summery vibe.

In a post shared on Instagram with her 49.5m followers, the supermodel gave fans a behind-the-scenes insight into her glam squad situ, which saw Hailey talking on the phone while her makeup artist created her eyeshadow look using – as eagle-eyed beauty obsessives will note – Kylie Cosmetics' Kyshadow Pressed Powder palette in the shade 'Bronze Nudes'.

Even before she has finished getting all glammed up, Hailey can't help looking nothing short of extraordinary, and, frankly, we've never seen anyone pull off the hair rollers look with such panache. How on earth she does it is one of life's great mysteries…

But the real scene-stealer in the series of photos was Hailey's playful one-shoulder t-shirt which depicted a cartoon woman's body sporting a bright pink crochet bikini and in the words of Gwyneth Paltrow, "Why not"?

The Hollywood actress was quick to comment on Hailey's joke t-shirt, offering her razor-sharp perspective on the supermodel's latest style moment. Clearly Gwynnie is a fan, and guess what? She wasn't alone in her appreciation for Hailey's unconventional summer bod, with model Lily Aldridge chiming in, writing "I’m obsessssed with this shirt".

Summer nostalgia-induced delusion? Perhaps, but we also kind of want one.
