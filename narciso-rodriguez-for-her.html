The history of Narciso Rodriguez for Her perfume

From Lancôme Juicy Tubes to Maybelline's Great Lash Mascara, there is a grand nostalgia surrounding early 2000s beauty. The decade synonymous with frosty lipstick, purple eyeshadow and skinny brows is seeing a reincarnation in modern make-up, following the resurgence of Y2K fashion in recent years, with post-'90s products proving just as popular today as they did back then. When it comes to perfumery, the story runs parallel, with one particular '00s fragrance boasting unwavering adoration: Narciso Rodriguez's For Her.

Two decades on from its 2003 launch, one bottle of For Her still sells every 15 seconds worldwide – and after its Musc Noir Rose iteration went viral on TikTok this year, it experienced a renewed surge in interest. An interaction between beauty influencer @ohuprettythings and a consumer in Paris resulted in the video in question, titled 'The French Girl perfume?', and it has since accumulated 2.9 million likes and 15.9 million views. With a powerhouse duo of perfumers (Christine Nagel and Francis Kurkdjian no less) behind its composition, it comes as no surprise that this cult floral woody musk is as popular today as it was two decades prior.

For the perfume's 20th anniversary, we – alongside renowned American designer Narciso Rodriguez and perfumer Sonia Constant – look retrospectively at For Her's creation, legacy and why this unique perfume remains a staple in scent wardrobes today.

"I wanted to create a fragrance that was addictive, sensual and eternally beautiful," Narciso Rodriguez tells Bazaar. "It also had to be a scent that was unique, something that celebrated women, their beauty and both their strength and their vulnerability."

The American designer, whose architectural, feminine clothes have been seen on A-listers from Michelle Obama to Kate Winslet and Sarah Jessica Parker, tells us that the initial inspiration for the scent came to him instantly, citing his muse as a mystery woman who was very significant in his life. "Her personal fragrance was a certain musk oil that I recognised from the first day I met her and even then, I was intrigued by it," he says. "It was so unusual – and it stayed with me. I couldn’t get it out of my head, so I began to wear it myself as both a lucky charm and a remembrance of her. The idea was to capture this ineffable sense of mystery, immediacy, intimacy and sensuality."

Estonian fashion model Carmen Kass has also been credited for her influencer on the perfume's conception; a poised black and white portrait of her, originally created for Rodriguez' fashion campaign, served as inspiration for the spirit of For Her. The brand has said the image represents timeless grace and intimate etherealness.

"I like to think the women who wear For Her are full of life," Rodiguez says. "They are career women, they are mothers, they are wives. I am inspired by women who project strong personalities, who carry themselves with pride and radiate grace and beauty from the inside out. When one feels sexy, mysterious and strong – not matter what you’re doing or wearing – it gives you a power that is subliminal."

The concept for its grand glass decanter can be linked back to Rodriguez's first trip to Asia when he was 23, a play on the illusion of multi-layered transparency as seen in traditional Chinese snuff bottles.

So what does For Her actually smell like? In terms of its composition and notes, the perfume is a floral woody musk, originally created by Christine Nagel – who has since created the likes of Si by Giorgio Armani and Jo Malone's English Pear & Freesia – and Francis Kurkdjian, who as well as being the perfume creation director at Christian Dior Parfums also has his own house, Maison Francis Kurkdjian, boasting exceptional fragrances such as Baccarat Rouge 540. For Her has a fruity, floral, rose and peach pulp opening, a heart of musk and a warm, woody base of amberlyn and patchouli. Narciso Rodriguez's in-house perfumer Sonia Constant describes For Her as luminous, elegant and full of grace.

"Linked to the actual smell of skin, musk is one of the rare ingredients that creates a raw addiction," she says. "The quantity of musks is very high in For Her, so the addiction is immediate, like love at first sight. It's timeless because its structure has nothing in common with other perfumes: it hasn't been created via consumer tests – it is a true statement, very unique and singular. People often wonder what the smell is, as opposed to what this perfume is. They think the woman smells so good, and not that her perfume smells good."

Narciso Rodriguez

From the aforementioned renaissance of Musc Noir Rose, which has cultivated a total of 81.5 million views on TikTok, to its provocative play on purity with Pure Musc, Narciso Rodriguez's For Her family has grown significantly since the fragrance's original launch in 2003. For its 20th anniversary, Rodriguez launched a new iteration of the iconic For Her scent: For Her Forever, this time curated by Constant.

"I wanted to bring the light of a firework into the perfume," Constant says, assuring us that Forever maintains For Her's original musky chypre scent, but enhanced with notes of jasmine, tuberose and frangipani.

"It is an incredibly rewarding moment to be celebrating For Her and the 20 years of love it has received from women around the world," Rodriguez adds. "I imagine it is still relevant today because it was a fragrance inspired by women for women, and born from so much passion."

Now, Narciso is ending a decades-long wait by debuting his second fragrance creation, All of Me. Unlike For Her, it's a floral fragrance with geranium as the key player, but – perhaps unsurprisingly – it also has a generous dose of musk. "I was a teenager in the '70s" Rodriguez tells us, "when musk was so popular – we used to go into the city and get these tiny little bottles of it". Now, "I wear musk all the time, and layer it, so it's always at the core of scent for me," he adds. The sensuality of musk, and how it smells different on everyone, make it "magical" to him. Subtle spicing alongside the floral top notes and musk turn "something classic into something new and multifaceted" he tells us.

Courtesy of Narciso Rodriguez

This isn't to say he wanted to create something radical, quite the opposite actually. "For me a fragrance needs to have values. That it be timeless, that it be ageless but modern, that it be universal, have grace and dignity. Not a fragrance for today but one that becomes part of your life." And if the enduring success of For Her is anything to go by, he's on the right track.
