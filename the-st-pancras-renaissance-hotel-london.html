The St Pancras Renaissance Hotel, London Hotel review

Arguably one of the most beautiful hotels in London, the St Pancras Renaissance is now an iconic landmark in its own right. Dating back as far back as 1873, the hotel has a rich history that is woven into the beauty of staying there.

The hotel began life as the Midland Grand, before it was unfortunately shut down in 1935 and later reopened to become the stunning hotel that it is today. You might recognise the grand staircase from that infamous Spice Girls' Wannabe video, but also have probably spotted the hotel in scenes from films such as Bridget Jones' Diary and Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets. All worth an impromptu photo-shoot during your stay.

Accommodation

The St Pancras Renaissance Hotel is made up of 207 rooms and 38 historical Chambers Suites. We stayed in the beautiful Haywood Suite, which was as grand and as beautiful as we could have possibly hoped.

Feeling less like a hotel suite and more like an upmarket London apartment, the 150 square-metre space is made up of sky-high ceilings, beautiful Victorian finishes, a vast separate lounge area and the ability to have interconnecting bedrooms if you're staying with more people. With so much space and in such a beautiful setting, you really can make it your home away from home during your stay, no matter how short it may be.

The Royal Suite also deserves a mention if you are after something truly spectacular and on a bigger scale, with the dining area able to seat up to 22 guests. It's ideal if you're looking to entertain away from home.

Food and drink

There are a number of different options for dining within the hotel. From the Gilbert Scott – where you can get the best of British grub from the celebrated chef Marcus Wareing – to the more casual eatery of the Mi + Me or even the delicious in-room dining menus, you are spoilt for choice.

We'd recommend making the most of your stay and having at least one decadent meal in your lovely suite. The chicken tikka curry was delicious, as were the fishcakes, and you can't miss the Eggs Royale for breakfast. In the afternoon, cuddle up with a rich hot chocolate and stay warm on a rainy London day.

Perfect for

A luxurious, romantic stay in the heart of London.

Book it

St Pancras Renaissance Hotel (0207 841 3540; stpancraslondon.com), from £899 a night in the Haywood Suite.
