A Legally Blonde TV series is in the works

As if news of a third Legally Blonde film wasn't enough, we're now being treated to Elle Woods on the small screen as well: a TV spin-off of the popular franchise is now in the works, 23 years after the first film premiered in cinemas.

Based on Amanda Brown’s novel of the same name and released in 2001, the original Legally Blonde was a box office success, with Elle quickly entering the history books as one of Reese Witherspoon's most iconic roles. It led to a sequel – Legally Blonde 2: Red, White & Blonde – in 2003, which saw Elle, now a Harvard graduate, defending animal rights in Congress. Then there was a Broadway musical, and the announcement of a third film, written by Mindy Kaling. This TV series is the (hot pink) icing on the cake.

Here's everything we know about the series so far...

Shutterstock

What's the new Legally Blonde series about?

No plot details are available yet, and all the juiciest details are being kept under wraps for now. All we know so far is that the series will be set within the Legally Blonde world – and that there could potentially be more than one off-shoot.

One show is being written by Josh Schwartz and Stephanie Savage (former showrunners for Gosspi Girl) alongside Witherspoon herself. It's being produced by Schwartz and Savage via their Fake Empire banner, with Hello Sunshine – Witherspoon's own company – as well as the Legally Blonde movie franchise producer, Marc Platt, and Amazon MGM.

Rumour has it that Hello Sunshine and Amazon are looking to further expand the Legally Blonde universe with a second spin-off series, which is still in 'preliminary idea' stages.

Metro Goldwyn Mayer

Will Reese Witherspoon be back as Elle Woods?

Previously, Witherspoon said that starring in Legally Blonde completely transformed her life. "I was in England making The Importance Of Being Earnest when Legally Blonde came out in America. When I went home, my life had changed forever," she said on the Graham Norton Show. In celebration of the first film’s 20th anniversary in 2021, she said that playing Elle was "the role of a lifetime".

There’s no word yet whether Reese will reprise her role, but as she is an executive producer through Hello Sunshine, we're keeping our fingers crossed for a cameo, at the very least.

We don't have any news on the rest of the cast, either, but as Amazon MGM and Hello Sunshine produced the hit series Daisy Jones & the Six, we've got high hopes.

Rex Features

Where – and when – can I watch the Legally Blonde series?

We don't have any hint of a release date yet, but as the series is being produced by Amazon MGM, we'd expect it to air on Amazon Prime Video. Get ready to perfect your bend and snap...

We'll be updating this story with more details as we have them.
