Small single-engine plane lands on Long Island's Southern State parkway with pilots sustaining minor

A small, single-engine plane landed on the Southern State Parkway on Long Island Tuesday morning, with the pilot and one passenger sustaining minor injuries.

The aircraft landed on the highway at around 11.40 am at Exit 33 to Route 109, located just miles away from Republic Airport, authorities said. 

The pilot and co-pilot were aboard the plane when the incident happened. Both were transported to hospital and sustained minor injuries, as reported by the Post.

'The pilot reported engine failure while on approach to Republic Airport in Farmingdale, New York,' a spokesperson with the Federal Aviation Administration told DailyMail.com. 

All lanes of the Southern State were subject to closure in the area, with only one eastbound lane available as of 12:30 pm.
