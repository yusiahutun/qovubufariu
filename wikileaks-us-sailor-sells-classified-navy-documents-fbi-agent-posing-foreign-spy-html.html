The leaks keep coming: U.S. sailor sells classified Navy documents to FBI agent posing as foreign spy

A low-level U.S sailor passed classified documents to an FBI agent posing as a foreign intelligence official in exchange for cash, according to a search warrant. 

As the impact of the WikiLeaks saga continues, the U.S. Navy faces a security concern of its own after Reserve Intelligence Specialist 3rd Class Bryan Minkyu Martin was arrested for selling dozens of secret or top secret documents to an undercover agent.

Martin, 22, was taken into police custody last week but has not not yet been charged, while the Naval Criminal Investigative Service continues its inquiry.

Selling secrets: Navy Reserve Intelligence Specialist 3rd Class Bryan Minkyu Martin has been arrested for selling dozens of classified documents to an undercover FBI agent

According to a warrant filed in Eastern District Court in North Carolina by the NCIS, Martin accepted a total of $3,500 from an undercover FBI agent in exchange for 51 documents that were classified either secret or top secret.

Martin was paid $500 for secret documents in one meeting and received payments of $1,500 in cash for even more files at two later meetings, signing a receipt using a code name.

Martin, who began his naval career in 2006 and received top security clearance the following year, had bragged that he could be 'very valuable' as an inside source, according to the NCIS warrant.

In the initial November 15 meeting with the undercover FBI agent, Martin revealed his current assignment focused on Afghanistan, and  claimed he would one day work for the Defense Intelligence Agency, according to the warrant.

'Martin stated that over his prospective 15 to 20 year career, he could be very valuable,' the warrant says.

However, Paul O'Donnell, a spokesman for the NCIS, said investigators were confident no classified information was actually delivered to anyone not authorised to see it.

Fort Bragg: Martin was stationed at the huge U.S. military base in North Carolina prior to his arrest

It has not yet been determined if Martin has a lawyer and calls to a phone number listed for his parents in New York went unanswered.

According to court documents, investigators seized a laptop computer, a digital audio recorder, a cell phone and unspecified file folders from the hotel on Fort Bragg where Martin was living.

His arrest comes as the fallout continues from WikiLeaks' posting of 250,000 U.S. diplomatic cables onto its website.

The site's founder Julian Assange is reportedly to meet British police 'by consent' as WikiLeaks published a classified cable which listed locations whose loss would cause critical damage to U.S. interests.

The WikiLeaks files were allegedly provided by U.S. Army Private Bradley Manning whose security clearance was significantly lower than Martin's.
