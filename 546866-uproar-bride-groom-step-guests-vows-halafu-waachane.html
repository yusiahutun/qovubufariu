Uproar as Bride and Groom Step on Guests after Vows: &quot;Halafu Waachane

  • A video captured after a wedding showed the bride and groom leaving the church in an unusual fashion
  • Numerous women lay on the ground at the entrance, and the couple stepped on their backs
  • The clip caused an uproar on social media, with netizens joking that the same would happen to the couple if they divorce

A video of newlyweds stepping on a group of women at a church in Tanzania is raising eyebrows on social media.

Why did couple step on wedding guests?

In the viral clip shared via Instagram, the newlyweds - Humphrey and Monica - walked over the women's backs soon after taking vows at the church.

The women, numbering at least 10, lay side-by-side, face down on the ground, at the church's entrance.

Humphrey and Monica were then assisted to balance as they stepped on the women's backs. Some can be seen wincing in pain as they are stepped on.

While it is unclear if the women lying on the ground were related to Humphrey or Monica, the custom is popular in the Marquesas Islands of French Polynesia.

There, the relatives of the bride lay on the ground.

It is similar to the tradition of jumping the broom at weddings.

Reacting to the clip, social media users fretted at the custom, saying it was degrading despite the cultural symbolism it carries for newlyweds.
