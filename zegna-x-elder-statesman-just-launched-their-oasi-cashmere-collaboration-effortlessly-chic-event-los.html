They can, however, dress up for fancy fashion events – and a few dapper gents showed up to the unveiling of the Zegna x The Elder Statesman collection, which took place on a balmy night at the Richard Neutra-designed Linn Residence on the Hollywood Hills on September 6.

The event celebrated the partnership between Italian menswear label Zegna and Los Angeles-based The Elder Statesman, known for its beautiful cashmere creations, for a range of genderless pieces co-designed by Zegna’s artistic director Alessandro Sartori and The Elder Statesman founder and CEO Greg Chait.

They were both in attendance alongside actors James Marsden, nominated for an Emmy for his role in hit show Jury Duty, and Evan Peters, also nominated for his dark turn as a serial killer in Netflix series Dahmer. Celebrated actor Kyle Maclachlan, who sported a beautiful corduroy jacket paired with checkered trousers from the range, also made an appearance.

The surfer vibe of the collection complemented the rustic-chic beach chalet-inspired decor of the house, which offered sweeping views of Hollywood Boulevard, Sunset Boulevard and beyond.

“It has my favourite view in the city and it’s perfect for this as far as less is more, because it was built to last and it’s not huge and very cool,” said Chait during a chat before the event, comparing the beautiful structure to to the artisanal approach of the Oasi Cashmere project.

The laid-back event, which also attracted other celebrities such as actors John Cho and Joseph Lee, and model Jessica Hart, also featured a number of performers, including duo Faux Real, whose 80s-inspired tunes and fun dance moves got the crowd dancing till the wee hours.

Celebrations of the Oasi Cashmere project will continue with events in Chengdu, China, and Singapore, during the city state’s famous Formula One Grand Prix.
