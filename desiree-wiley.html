Desiree Wiley- Professional Career

Desiree abandoned beauty pageants to pursue a career in journalism, beginning as a reporter at WKBW-TV in Buffalo. She also worked as a fill-in anchor for the next three years. Desiree got the opportunity to demonstrate her abilities live as she covered breaking news such as the Clinton Correctional Facility escape (the case in which two inmates, David Sweat and Richard Matt, fled from the prison) in 2015 and the storm in Buffalo in November 2014. She subsequently went to work for NBC Boston and the NECN Network in 2016, when she was offered the role of weekend anchor, and she is still working there at the moment.

Desiree has also worked as an economics lecturer and as the editor of the magazine “The World of Economics.”

Desiree has been actively involved in charitable and social activities for over six years. She promoted the Upstate New York Transplant Service, which is an Organ Procurement Organization with offices in eight countries, including Canada and Cuba. Aside from these activities, Desiree had the opportunity to obtain some expertise in the sector of higher education when she worked as an admission advisor.
