A leaked video has shown the moment a prison captain pepper sprayed a restrained inmate in the face before taunting him and leaving him in distress for more than 20 minutes.

On the footage, Captain Shawn Welch sprays the irritant at Paul Schlosser after the man spat at another officer while he was being restrained at Maine Correctional Center in Windham, Maine.

The spray should be administered no nearer than six feet from the target, whereas Welch was just 18 inches from Schlosser. It also should be used on multiple people, rather than just one.

Welch then taunts him, daring him to cause more trouble, as Schlosser struggles to breathe and pleads for help and medical care.

'You're not going to win,' Welch is heard saying. 'I will win every time.'

The video has now been leaked, along with documents and interviews conducted for an investigation into the incident, and published in the Portland Press Herald and Maine Sunday Telegram.

Welch was fired from his job in August after the investigation was launched into the June 10 incident. It concluded that Welch violated prison policies and had a personal grudge against Schlosser.

But the decision was later overruled by Corrections Commissioner Joseph Ponte, who gave Welch a 30-day suspension instead, according to documents now seen by the Portland Press Herald.

Tied down: Schlosser was restrained after he cut himself and refused medical treatment. He had to be held down so medical staff could do their job. Before he was pepper sprayed, he spat at an officer

Combative: He was also filmed yelling his frustrations at an officer before the pepper spraying attack

As the video is leaked along with documents and interviews about the incident, there are questions over Welch, who is back in his role, did the right thing or whether he used excessive force.

'In my investigation it appears that the situation went from a security situation to a punishment one,' Scott Durst, a former Maine Drug Enforcement Agency detective, wrote in his report.

But Ponte said he looked at Welch's service before the incident and found he had a completely clean record during his years with the prison.

'When you've got a substantial amount of years of good, sound decision-making and performance measured against one bad decision, it's kind of, you look at the odds,' Ponte said.

Schlosser is serving seven years for a 2010 robbery spree sparked by a search for drug money.

Holding on: Welch, left, was initially fired for the incident and then this was overturned and reduced to a suspension due to his previous solid service. Schlosser is pictured in his mug shot, right

He had been a medic in the military before 2008 and now takes medication for bipolar disorder and depression. Before the video incident, he had cut his arms and been taken to hospital.

He told the Press Herald in an interview that he had hurt himself because he was depressed over the break up of his marriage and how he was kept separate from the general inmate population.

When he returned to his cell, he removed the dressing and was put on the restraint chair, which is rarely used in prisons, so that medical staff could treat his wounds.

In the video, when he spits at an officer from the chair, Welch, who is standing by his feet, sprays his face. He said it was because he was aware that the inmate had Hepatitis C.

A spit mask is then put over Schlosser's face so the spray cannot evaporate. Welch proceeds to taunt Schlosser, warning him that if he doesn't co-operate, he will be sprayed again.

Shock: Schlosser's mother and sister react as they watch the video of his treatment in prison

'Certainly from an outsider's point of view it looks horrific,' Welch himself said, according to the reports shared in the Press Herald.

The investigator concluded that Welch continued to 'brow beat' Schlosser, suggesting it was personal. He also noted that Welch escalated the situation, rather than attempt to stop it.

Brian MacMaster, head of investigations for the Maine Attorney General's Office, said the video did not appear criminal but did violate several policies, the Press Herald reported.

Welch has simply been given a performance improvement plan to address the policies he violated, but he will not undergo any training as he is already an 'expert' in the policies he violated.

The Maine Department of Corrections is now also investigating how the press obtained the video and documents.
