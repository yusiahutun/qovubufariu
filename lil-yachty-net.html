Biography 

Early Life Lil Yachty

Born Miles Parks McCollum in Mableton, Georgia, on August 23, 1997, Lil Yachty loved music from a young age. Early inspirations, especially Drake, shaped his musical goals.

Lil Yachty temporarily attended Alabama State University in 2015 but dropped out to focus on music. He moved to New York to follow his aspirations and immersed himself in the music and streetwear worlds. He prioritised industry networking and web presence throughout this time.

Understanding the power of social media, Lil Yachty actively worked on growing his Instagram following, showcasing his entrepreneurial spirit. He worked at McDonald’s to sustain himself during this time, showing his dedication to music. Lil Yachty’s hip-hop breakthrough began with networking, hard effort, and clever social media use.

Career

After a viral video made “One Night” popular, Lil Yachty’s music career took off. The catchy song became popular, sparking interest in the singer.

Modeling for Kanye West’s Yeezy line in 2016 boosted Lil Yachty’s career. “Lil Boat,” which includes “One Night,” launched his successful career. The mixtape’s success led to collaborations with famous rappers and musicians who saw Lil Yachty’s potential.

Lil Yachty’s “Broccoli” collaboration with DRAM and Chance the Rapper secured his music career. On his second mixtape, “Summer Songs 2,” he collaborated with Kyle and Tee Grizzley to keep his momentum.

Lil Yachty’s first studio album, “Teenage Emotions,” debuted at number five on the Billboard Top 200 in 2017. Migos, Diplo, and YG collaborated on the album.

His second studio album, “Lil Boat 2,” debuted at number two on the Billboard Top 200 in 2018. The album’s financial success showed Lil Yachty’s hip-hop appeal and unique style despite varied reviews.

Performing “If You Were Mine” with Ocean Park Standoff in 2018, Lil Yachty expanded his musical repertoire. Yachty announced “Lil Boat 3.” in 2020 after a quiet 2019. The album’s lead song, “Oprah’s Bank Account,” featured Drake, Lil Yachty’s childhood idol.

Yachty’s October 2022 non-album song “Poland,” went viral. His fifth studio album, “Let’s Start Here,” released in January 2023, straying from trap and incorporating psychedelic rock.

Lil Yachty’s variety, collaboration, and artistic growth have made him a notable figure in current hip-hop.

Musical style

Lil Yachty calls his music “bubblegum trap.” His unique sound draws from Mario Bros., Charlie Brown, the Rugrats theme, the GameCube system launch sound, and J-pop artist Daoko. His artwork also features clouds, cotton candy, the Super Nintendo, and Pixar motifs. His tunes are generally produced alongside his pal TheGoodPerry.

Mumble rap is another genre that fits his music. Rolling Stone called his music “catchy, intentionally dinky-sounding tunes packed with off-color boasts delivered in a proudly amateurish singsong.” The Guardian called Lil Yachty’s music “fun, hook-first pop rap oblivious to songcraft and structure that doesn’t take itself too seriously, with very little interest in legacy and even less in rap canon.” This blend of influences informs Lil Yachty’s unorthodox hip hop style.
